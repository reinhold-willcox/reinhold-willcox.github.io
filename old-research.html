About

I am currently working towards a PhD in Theoretical and Compuational Astrophysics at Monash University in Melbourne, AU. My focus is on interacting massive binary stars, the kinds of things which may become merging double compact objects which could be observed by the LIGO-Virgo-KAGRA gravitational wave detectors. However, binary star evolution can be very uncertain, and merging double compact objects are a very rare evolutionary endpoint.

As a member and core developer of the Team COMPAS, I use rapid binary population synthesis (or popsynth) to sample a large parameter space in the binary initial conditions and quickly model the evolution using approximate parametrizations to more detailed physics. This approach allows us to determine which evolutionary parameters have the greatest impact on later stages, and which are the most uncertain.

My research specifically looks the effects of binary stripping on supernova explosions, and how uncertainties even in the initial masses and periods of the binaries could be having a larger impact than previously considered. See here for an updated list of publications.

My expected completion date is Nov. 2022, and I am actively seeking out post-doctoral opportunities and willing to travel internationally. If you know of an opportunity or would like to chat further, please reach out.
